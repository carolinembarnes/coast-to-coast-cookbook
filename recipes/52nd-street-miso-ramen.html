52nd Street Miso Ramen

Experiment (Not Final) Main Dish Noodles Soup

Add your story or memory about this ramen here! For example: Inspired by late nights and comfort food cravings, this ramen blends miso broth, rotisserie chicken, and customizable toppings for a cozy, east-meets-west flavor.

Ingredients

Broth

  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 scallions, white and light green, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, peeled and chopped
  • 1” knob fresh ginger, peeled and sliced
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp chili oil
  • 3 tbsp white miso paste
  • ⅓ cup mirin
  • 2 (32oz) boxes low sodium chicken broth
  • 2 strips of bacon

Toppings

  • 1 rotisserie chicken, picked and shredded
  • 8oz Chinese noodles, cooked
  • 4 eggs, soft boiled
  • 1 cup corn, fresh or frozen (thawed)
  • 1 cup arugula or spinach
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms, sautéed
  • 1 cup shredded carrots (optional)
  • 1 cup bean sprouts (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil in a dutch oven (or heavy-bottom pan) over medium-high heat. Whisk together scallion whites, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, chili oil, miso paste, mirin, and ¼ cup of the chicken stock. Simmer for 3-5 minutes until all the ingredients create a thick sauce.
  2. Add remaining broth and scallion greens (optional: add bacon here), and simmer for 10-20 minutes. Strain broth, removing scallions, garlic, and ginger.
  3. While the broth is simmering, cook the noodles according to package directions. Prepare soft-boiled eggs in the Instant Pot, and additional toppings.
  4. To assemble, divide noodles into bowls. Pour two cups of broth over the noodles. Add desired toppings, including chicken, corn, egg, arugula, mushrooms, etc.
