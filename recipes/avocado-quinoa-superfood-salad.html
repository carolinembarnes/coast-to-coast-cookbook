Avocado & Quinoa Superfood Salad

Lunch Experiment (Not Final) Salad Vegetarian Superfood Summer

A nutrient-packed, colorful salad featuring crispy quinoa, fresh herbs, creamy avocado, and a smoky chipotle vinaigrette—a summer favorite for lunch or potlucks.

Ingredients

Salad Base

  • 1 cup quinoa, uncooked
  • 2 tbsp oil, olive or canola
  • 1 cup corn, roasted and cut from cob
  • 1 red onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 quart cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup cilantro
  • ½ cup fresh basil
  • 10 oz romaine, chopped
  • 6 oz curly kale, chopped
  • 15 oz black beans, drained
  • 1 cup jicama, thinly sliced
  • 4 oz feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 avocado

Chipotle Vinaigrette

  • ½ cup red wine vinegar
  • ⅓ cup honey
  • 1 tbsp adobo sauce
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp fresh oregano leaves
  • 1½ cups olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan of salted boiling water, cook the quinoa until just tender, about 12 minutes. Drain well in a fine sieve and spread on a rimmed baking sheet. Let stand, tossing occasionally, until cooled and dry, about 30 minutes.
  2. While the quinoa is cooling, combine corn, red onion, bell pepper, tomatoes, cilantro, and basil in a large bowl. Toss to combine. Add in romaine, kale, and black beans. Toss again.
  3. In a medium skillet, heat 1/4 inch of oil until it shimmers. Add the quinoa and fry over moderate heat, stirring, until the sizzling subsides and the quinoa is crisp, about 2 minutes. Drain the quinoa and transfer to paper towels. Season with salt.
  4. In a food processor or blender, combine red wine vinegar, honey, adobo sauce, water, cumin, garlic powder, and oregano. Turn on the processor. With the motor running, drizzle in the oil until the salad dressing comes together. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  5. Top salad mixture with jicama, feta, and avocado. Sprinkle it with crispy quinoa and drizzle with chipotle dressing.
