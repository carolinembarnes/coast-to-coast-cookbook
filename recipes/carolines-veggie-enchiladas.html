Caroline's Veggie Enchiladas

Main Dish Vegetarian Final

Add your story or inspiration here! For example: These enchiladas are packed with roasted sweet potatoes, poblanos, and melty cheese—a crowd-pleaser for veggie lovers and skeptics alike.

Ingredients

  • 2 sweet potatoes, diced
  • ½ tsp chipotle powder
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 poblano peppers
  • 8 flour tortillas (soft taco size works well)
  • 19 oz enchilada sauce, mild red
  • 16 oz refried black beans
  • 8 oz pepper jack cheese, shredded
  • 8 oz Mexican cheese blend, shredded
  • 2 scallions, white and light green parts chopped
  • Cilantro, for serving
  • Sour cream, for serving
  • Avocado, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (205°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. On the prepared baking sheet, toss sweet potatoes with salt, chipotle powder, and olive oil. Arrange the potatoes in a single layer, spacing them apart. Rub the poblano peppers with olive oil and place, whole, on the baking sheet. Bake for 35 to 45 minutes until potatoes are soft. Once cooled, remove skin, stem, and seeds from peppers and dice, then mix with sweet potatoes.
  3. Turn the oven down to 350°F (175°C). Spread 1 cup of enchilada sauce in a 9x13 baking dish.
  4. Combine the pepper jack and Mexican shredded cheeses. Set up an assembly line of black beans, sweet potato and pepper mix, cheese mix, and chopped scallions. Fill each tortilla with equal parts beans and sweet potato mix, top with a handful of cheese and a sprinkle of scallions. Fold and place in the baking dish, seam side down.
  5. Pour the remaining enchilada sauce on top of the tortillas and sprinkle the remaining cheese mix on top. Bake uncovered for 20 to 25 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Serve hot, topped with fresh cilantro, sour cream, and avocado as desired.
