Mama’s Chicken Soup

Main Dish Comfort Food Family Favorite

This soup is a staple in our family, passed down from husband's grandmother and always made with love. Whether you’re feeling under the weather or just want something warm and comforting, this recipe brings together simple ingredients and memories from the east coast.

Ingredients

  • 1 rotisserie chicken
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 3 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 russet potatoes, cut into bite sized pieces
  • 3 cans of low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 cans of cream of chicken soup, preferably low sodium
  • ½ cup of (partially) cooked white rice

Instructions

  1. In a dutch oven, bring chicken to a boil in 4 cups of water. Turn heat to medium-high and simmer for 10 minutes. Remove large chicken pieces and strain remaining broth back into the dutch oven.
  2. Add carrots, celery, and potatoes to broth and simmer on medium heat until soft. Add chicken broth and an additional can of water, and bring to a slow boil.
  3. Remove chicken skin and bones from meat. Shred chicken before returning to dutch oven with vegetables and broth over medium-low heat. Add partially cooked rice and simmer for 1-4 hours, as desired.
  4. Add can(s) of cream of chicken soup and additional chicken broth to reach desired consistency. Serve hot.
