Too Easy Chicken Perlo

Main Dish Final Family Recipe Chicken Southern

My grandmother used to make this for me growing up—a comforting Southern casserole that comes together with simple ingredients and big flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 rotisserie chicken
  • 2 cups of long-grain white rice
  • 1 can of condensed cream of chicken soup
  • ½ cup yellow onion, diced
  • ½ cup celery, diced
  • 3 eggs, hard boiled and chopped
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Coat a 9x13 baking dish with butter.
  2. Bring whole chicken to a boil in 4 cups of water. Turn heat to medium-high and simmer for 10 minutes. Remove large chicken pieces and strain remaining broth. Debone and shred chicken.
  3. Add 2 cups of rice to strained broth and bring to a boil. Turn the heat down to medium-low and simmer for 20 minutes until rice is fully cooked.
  4. Mix all ingredients together in a large mixing bowl. Pour into the prepared pan.
  5. Bake for 30 minutes uncovered or until the casserole is lightly browned.

Another Idea...

Remove gizzards and rinse the whole chicken inside and out. Peel and slice two onions, cutting a grid into each onion from the top but stopping short of the bottom so the onion remains whole. Slice celery (and carrots, if using) on an angle into large pieces. Peel and crush garlic cloves. Chop fresh herbs (basil, oregano, rosemary, and thyme). Prepare 2 tablespoons of salt and 1 tablespoon of pepper, season to taste.

Place chicken, onion, celery, and herbs in a large pot and pour in enough water to cover the chicken. Bring to a boil and cook until chicken is tender, about one hour. Remove the chicken and set aside to cool. Remove all large pieces of vegetables and garlic—save or discard as you wish.

Remove the skin and bones from the chicken. Chop or pull chicken into bite-size pieces.

Place chicken pieces, rice (Carolina Gold is recommended), and dried Italian-style seasoning in the pot of chicken stock. Cook together, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and cover. Cook for 20-30 minutes until the rice is tender, stirring often.
